  1. What is Triangular Trade?

    The triangular trade system was a triangle shaped trade route used by the Europians in the 16th century. The route was between Europe, Africa and America, which formed a triangle from the top view. The direction was Europe, Africa, America, then back to Europe.

  2. System of the route

    A system is a multiple section and parts that make up a specific flow or function. The triangular trade system is also an example of a system, formed by European colonizers, Slaves, Materials, Goods, Old World, and New World.
    In the trade route system, goods, materials, people, diseases, animals, and plants were transported along the triangle. Some materials and goods were carried from Europe to Africa, slaves were transferred from Africa to America, and raw materials were moved to Europe from America.

  3. Things spread around the world

    In the triangular trade system, many things were spread to the New World from the Old World, Old World to the New World.
    Some of the things spread from the Old World to New World were wheat, sugar, rice, coffee, horses, cows, pigs, sheep, chickens, and diseases such as smallpox and measles.
    Things spread from the New World to Old World were potatoes, tomatoes, peppers, tobaccos and other raw materials.

  4. Conclusion

    In conclusion, the triangular trade system was a significant trade route in the 16th century that connected Europe, Africa, and America. It developed the trade of goods, materials, people, diseases, animals, and plants.
    Lastly, I am thinking that this system was fair. It is because I am thinking that the cycle and functionality of the route is well made. However, slavery in current value can be unfair. In terms of developement, it helped the advance of America and Old World by spread of materials and people.

    Mini Informations

    1. Smallpox and Measles

      Smallpox and measles were two of the most popular diseases brought to the New World in 16th century, and it largly affected the population of indigenous people. Smallpox were very easy to spread and were fatal, causing large decrease of Native Americans. The spread of smallpox and measles is one of the worst consequences of colonization of America. Now, smallpox were eradicated in 1980 by WHO, while measles still exist.

    2. Sugar Profit

      Sugar was one of the most profitable goods in the triangular trade system. It was produced in America by slaves, and transported to Europe. In Europe, sugar was highly popular which made huge profit for the Europeans, and it was one of the largest reasons for the continuation of the triangular trade.

    3. African Slaves

      Triangular trade transported slaves to America which supported the developement of the slave trade. They were captured and enslaved in Africa and transported to America, and they were forced to work in plantations and mines.
