  1. What is Triangular Trade?

    The triangular trade system was a triangular trade route used by the Europians in the 16th century. The route was between Europe, Africa and America, which formed a triangle from the top view. The direction was Europe, Africa, America, then back to Europe.

  2. How was the route like?

    In the trade route, materials, goods, and people were transported along the triangle. Some materials and goods were carried from Europe to Africa, slaves were transferred from Africa to America, and raw materials were moved to Europe from America.

  3. Things spread around the world

    In the triangular trade system, many things were spread to the New World from the Old World.
    Some of the things spread from the Old World were wheat, sugar, rice, coffee, horses, cows, pigs, sheep, chickens, and diseases such as smallpox and measles.
